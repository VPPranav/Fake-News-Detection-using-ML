Learn how to identify fake news and protect yourself from misinformation.

How to Spot Fake News

  • Check the source's credibility
  • Look for unusual URLs or site names
  • Check the "About Us" section
  • Look for excessive ads or clickbait
  • Check the article date and time
  • Look for supporting sources
  • Check if other news outlets are reporting it
  • Beware of confirmation bias

Red Flags in News Articles

  • ALL CAPS headlines or excessive punctuation!!!
  • Emotional language designed to provoke outrage
  • Claims of "they don't want you to know this"
  • No author attribution or anonymous sources
  • Poor grammar or spelling errors
  • Manipulated or out-of-context images
  • Lack of specific dates, locations, or details
  • Extraordinary claims without evidence

Cognitive Biases to Watch For

  • Confirmation Bias: Seeking information that confirms existing beliefs
  • Availability Bias: Overestimating the importance of information that comes to mind quickly
  • Bandwagon Effect: Believing something because many others do
  • Negativity Bias: Giving more weight to negative news
  • Anchoring Bias: Relying too heavily on the first piece of information
